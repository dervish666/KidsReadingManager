# Data Subject Rights

**Tally** (trading as Tally Reading)

**Last updated:** 24 June 2026

---

## 1. Overview

This document explains how Tally Reading ("Tally") supports the rights of
individuals ("data subjects") under the UK GDPR.

Tally acts as a **data processor** on behalf of schools, which are the **data
controllers**. Data subjects are students (children aged 4–11), school staff,
and — through the school — parents and carers.

Because the data subjects are largely children, their rights are exercised by
the school as controller. **Tally does not accept rights requests directly from
parents or students; all requests come through the school.** Staff may request
their own data through their school or directly to Tally.

Requests are made by an authenticated school administrator or owner, or by email
to **privacy@tallyreading.uk** from a verified school contact. Tally verifies
that each request comes from an authorised school account before acting;
verifying the identity of a parent or carer is the school's responsibility as
controller.

---

## 2. Right of Access (Article 15)

On a school's request, Tally provides all personal data held about an identified
student or staff member in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).
For a student this includes their profile (name, class, year group, reading-level
range, notes), reading history, genre preferences and streaks. For staff it
includes their account details and activity records. Global book-catalogue data,
other individuals' data and internal system data are not personal data of the
subject and are not included.

**Timeline:** Tally acknowledges within 1 business day and provides the export to
the school within 5 business days — well inside the school's one-month statutory
deadline.

---

## 3. Right to Rectification (Article 16)

Most data can be corrected immediately by the school through the Tally dashboard
(student details, reading levels, session records, genre preferences and staff
account details). Where a correction cannot be made through the interface, the
school contacts Tally support; Tally verifies the request and makes the
correction directly, with the change captured in the audit log. Support-assisted
corrections are completed within 3 business days.

---

## 4. Right to Erasure (Article 17)

On a school's instruction, Tally permanently deletes (hard delete) the relevant
personal data from its live systems:

- **A student:** their record and all associated reading sessions, preferences
  and streaks are permanently removed.
- **A staff member:** their account and associated authentication data are
  permanently removed; audit-log references are anonymised so the accountability
  trail remains intact without identifying the individual.
- **A whole school:** Tally can purge an entire organisation, permanently
  deleting all of its data and replacing the organisation record with an
  anonymised tombstone.

Erasure is effective immediately in the live database. Cached AI-recommendation
data referencing the subject expires automatically within 7 days. Tally
acknowledges within 1 business day and confirms completion within 30 calendar
days. Erasure may be declined only where retention is required by law (for
example, to establish or defend legal claims).

---

## 5. Right to Restriction (Article 18)

Where a school asks Tally to restrict processing — for example, while the
accuracy of data is contested — Tally will, on the controller's instruction,
exclude the relevant records from active processing (reports, class lists and AI
recommendations) while retaining them, and will lift the restriction when the
school confirms the grounds no longer apply.

---

## 6. Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

Tally provides a student's or staff member's data in a structured, commonly used,
machine-readable format (JSON or CSV) for transfer to the school or another
system. Exports are delivered to the school via a secure channel within
5 business days.

---

## 7. Right to Object (Article 21)

Core reading-tracking is necessary for the school's use of the service and is not
subject to objection. The optional AI book-recommendation feature involves
profiling; where a school notifies Tally that a parent objects, Tally disables AI
recommendations for that student while leaving all other features available. (AI
recommendations are off by default and are enabled only at the school's choice.)

---

## 8. Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

**Tally does not make automated decisions that produce legal or similarly
significant effects.** The only automated processing is AI-generated book
_suggestions_, which are always reviewed by a teacher who makes the final choice.
Recommendations have no bearing on grades, class placement or any other
significant outcome. Data sent to AI providers is limited to pseudonymised
reading-profile information — no student names or identifying details are sent.

---

## 9. Complaints

If a school or individual is dissatisfied with how a request has been handled,
they may contact Tally at **privacy@tallyreading.uk**. Data subjects also have
the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

- Website: ico.org.uk
- Telephone: 0303 123 1113
- Address: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane,
  Wilmslow, Cheshire SK9 5AF
