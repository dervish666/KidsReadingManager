# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are **reading volunteers** and **teachers** at UK primary schools. They use the app on **iPads**, often balanced on small chairs during 20-minute reading sessions with children aged 5-11. Many volunteers are older adults or parents who are not particularly tech-confident. The app must feel instantly usable without training. Secondary users are school admins who manage classes, students, and settings. The job to be done: track reading sessions, discover appropriate books, and monitor student reading progress — replacing paper-based systems and spreadsheets.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, Practical, Caring** — like a trusted teaching assistant who quietly makes everything easier. The voice is understated British: friendly but not patronising, helpful without being showy. Copy should feel human and grounded ("No spreadsheets required", "The whole class at a glance"). The emotional goal is **quiet confidence** — users should feel that the tool has their back, that children's data is safe, and that nothing will go wrong in front of a child.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Cozy bookshelf — warm creams, sage greens, and soft earth tones. Inviting rather than clinical. The design should feel like a well-loved school library corner, not an enterprise dashboard.
- **Theme**: Light mode only. School iPads are used in bright classrooms where dark mode adds no value.
- **Current direction is right**: The existing "Cozy Bookshelf" theme with glassmorphism surfaces, warm shadows, and rounded shapes is the target aesthetic. Evolution, not revolution.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid corporate SaaS aesthetics (sharp corners, blue/grey palettes, dense information panels). Avoid overly playful children's app aesthetics (bright primary colours, cartoon illustrations). The app is for adults working with children, not for children directly.

### Design System (from codebase)

**Colour Palette**:
| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Sage Green (primary) | `#6B8E6B` | Buttons, links, active states, logo background |
| Warm Brown (secondary) | `#8B7355` | Accents, bookshelf elements, shadow tints |
| Warm Cream (background) | `#F5F0E8` | Page background |
| Ivory (surface) | `rgba(255, 254, 249, 0.85-0.95)` | Cards, papers (with backdrop-blur) |
| Dark Grey (text primary) | `#4A4A4A` | Headings, primary text |
| Warm Grey (text secondary) | `#7A7A7A` | Body text, labels |
| Muted Red (status) | `#9E4B4B` | Not read / overdue status |
| Amber (status) | `#9B6E3A` | Needs attention status |
| Status Green | `#4A6E4A` | Recently read status |
| Coral | `#C47C5A` | Landing page accent (feature tags) |
| Muted Teal (info) | `#7A9EAD` | Info states |

**Typography**:
| Context | Font | Weight | Notes |
|---------|------|--------|-------|
| App headings (h1-h6) | Nunito | 700-900 | Rounded sans-serif, warm personality |
| App body + buttons | DM Sans | 400-700 | Clean, readable, slightly geometric |
| Landing page headings | Fraunces | 600-700 | Serif with character, literary feel |

**Surface Treatment**:
- Glassmorphism: translucent backgrounds with `backdrop-filter: blur(16-20px)`
- Warm shadow palette: `rgba(139, 115, 85, 0.08-0.15)` — never pure black shadows
- Border radius: 10-16px (cards/papers), 50px (pills/buttons on landing)
- Subtle borders: `rgba(139, 115, 85, 0.08-0.12)` — barely visible structural lines

**Motion**:
- Card hover: `translateY(-4px)` with shadow expansion (desktop pointer only)
- Button active: `scale(0.97-0.98)` for tactile feel
- Scroll reveals: `translateY(24px)` fade-up, 0.6s ease
- Background: Slow floating blobs (8-10s ease-in-out infinite)
- Transitions: `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` for material-style easing

**Component Patterns**:
- Custom illustrated navigation icons (PNG) in bottom nav
- Bookshelf decorative borders (desktop only, hidden on tablet/mobile)
- Status indicators: small coloured dots with inset shadow for depth
- Streak badges: flame icon with count
- Book covers: OpenLibrary images with deterministic gradient placeholders from title hash

### Design Principles

1. **Invisible until needed** — The interface should disappear during a reading session. Nothing should distract from the child and the book. Controls appear when needed and recede when they don't.

2. **Big enough to tap without thinking** — Touch targets must be generous (minimum 44px, prefer 48px+). Volunteers shouldn't have to concentrate on hitting a button while a child reads aloud. Prioritise large, well-spaced interactive elements.

3. **Warm, never clinical** — Every surface, shadow, and colour choice should feel like a cozy reading nook, not a hospital dashboard. Prefer cream over white, sage over blue, rounded over sharp.

4. **One glance, full picture** — Key information (who needs reading, reading status, session counts) should be scannable without interaction. Use colour, position, and size to create natural visual hierarchy.

5. **Trust through simplicity** — Schools trust tools that feel simple and safe. Avoid dense UIs, complex options, or anything that makes users worry they'll break something. Every screen should feel manageable.
